Discussion 3


An artist who I have always admired is Jon McCormack. His work reminds me of more of an animation, or digitally animated photos together. But upon doing some further research, his work consists of methods that involve "algorithmically 'grown' ideas from computer code using artificial evolution and generative grammars." One piece that stands out is Fifty Sisters, FIFTY SISTERS which consists of repetitive flowers arranged to form another flower. Fifty Sisters is a large-scale installation of 1m x 1m images of computer synthesized plant-forms. I really looked at is as an artwork that must have been created in illustrator or Photoshop by duplicating the images over and over again and realigning them. But then after researching more, he generated this work by not only looping certain commands, but also added variables to relocate each flower.

Each flower has been created with graphic elements of oil company logos, which all together combine as "sisters".
